🧑‍🌾 Farmer Story
Le Thi Phuong
Phuong Mountain Cacao · Gia Lai Province · 2.8 hectares

Le Thi Phuong has grown cacao on the same hillside plot in Gia Lai for over fifteen years. He inherited the land from his father, who originally planted coffee and pepper before Hoa converted the slopes to cacao in 2009 — a gamble that has since defined his family's livelihood.

Hoa is known among our partners for his patience during fermentation. He refuses to cut the process short even when market conditions favour faster throughput, believing that six full days in the wooden box is the only way to unlock the complexity the Trinitario hybrid grown at this altitude can deliver.

His farm sits at 850 metres — high enough for cooler nights that slow the cacao's growth and concentrate its flavour, but sheltered enough from the highland winds to allow consistent yields through the January harvest season.
