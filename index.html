The Question Everyone Asks

What is ceremonial cacao?

Ceremonial cacao is 100% pure cacao paste made from whole fermented cacao beans — minimally processed, with nothing added and nothing removed. It is fundamentally different from cocoa powder or chocolate, which are heavily refined, stripped of nutrients, and often loaded with sugar and additives.

For thousands of years, Mesoamerican cultures used raw cacao in rituals, ceremonies, and as daily medicine. What we offer is that same ancient food — brought to you from the highlands of Vietnam.

We're often asked if our cacao is certified organic. The honest answer: our cacao is grown organically — but it's not officially certified, and here's why that's actually a sign of integrity.

Our farmers are small family operations in Dak Lak and other highland provinces of Vietnam. For remote smallholders like these, third-party organic certification is logistically difficult and financially out of reach — it's a system built for large co-ops with capital and infrastructure, not for independent farming families growing a few hectares in the hills.

What we can tell you is this: our partner farmers don't use synthetic pesticides or fertilizers. Their methods are rooted in traditional Vietnamese farming practices that have sustained healthy soil for generations. In many of these growing areas, chemical inputs simply aren't part of the culture — or the budget.

At kakao.guru, our trust isn't built on a label. It's built on direct relationships with our farmers, full traceability through our QR system, and a commitment to transparency about exactly where your cacao comes from.
