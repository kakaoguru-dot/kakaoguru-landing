Classic Oat Cacao Latte
The one most people start with. Creamy, earthy, naturally sweet.
☀️ Morning🌙 EveningBeginner
Ingredients
Kakao.guru cacao block20g
Oat milk220ml
Cinnamonsmall pinch
Method
1
Shave 20g cacao into thin pieces.
2
Heat oat milk to 70°C.
3
Blend together for 30 seconds until smooth and frothy.
4
Dust with cinnamon. Drink immediately.
The most honest version. No sweetener needed — oat milk's natural sweetness is enough.

Golden Cacao
Anti-inflammatory, deeply warming. Turmeric meets cacao.
🌙 EveningAnti-inflammatory
Ingredients
Cacao block15g
Coconut milk200ml
Turmeric¼ tsp
Cinnamon¼ tsp
Black pepperpinch
Method
1
Warm coconut milk gently with turmeric and cinnamon — don't boil.
2
Add shaved cacao and black pepper.
3
Blend until fully emulsified and golden.
Black pepper is essential here — it dramatically increases turmeric's bioavailability. Coconut milk's fat also helps absorption.

Iced Cacao Latte
Smooth, cold, surprisingly refreshing. The summer version.

Pure Black Cacao
No milk. Just cacao and water. For the purists.
⚡ IntenseCeremonialPurist
Ingredients
Cacao block20–40g
Hot water200ml
Method
1
Shave cacao into a mug or blender.
2
Pour 70°C water over the cacao.
3
Blend 45 seconds until fully emulsified and frothy.
4
Drink slowly. The flavour is intense — complex, bitter, earthy.
This is the most honest way to experience the Vietnam origin. No sweetness, no fat to soften it. Drink it in ceremony.

Tips for the perfect cup

🌡️
Temperature matters
70°C is the sweet spot. Boiling water (100°C) can damage delicate aromatic compounds. Let your kettle cool for 2 minutes after boiling.
⚖️
Weigh your cacao
A kitchen scale changes everything. 20g is very different from 15g or 30g — especially in terms of how you feel. Find your dose and stick to it.
🌀
Blend, don't just stir
Cacao contains fat (cacao butter). Blending emulsifies the fat into the liquid, creating a genuinely smooth texture. Stirring alone won't achieve this.
🥛
Milk choice matters
Oat milk: naturally sweet, everyday. Coconut milk: rich and creamy, evening/ceremonial. Almond milk: light and neutral. Water: pure, intense.
🧂
A pinch of salt
A tiny pinch of sea salt (literally just a few grains) deepens the chocolate flavour and reduces perceived bitterness. Try it once and you won't stop.
⏰
Give it 20 minutes
Theobromine takes 20–30 minutes to reach full effect. Don't judge your cacao in the first 10 minutes. Sit with it, and let it work.
